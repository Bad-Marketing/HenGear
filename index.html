Q3 2026 · Amazon Growth Strategy

HENGEAR Q3 2026

The Leading Roll-Away Nesting Box Brand on Amazon

Prepared by BAD Marketing  ·  Amazon Department

01Strategic Focus

Continue establishing HenGear as the leading roll-away nesting box brand on Amazon by expanding non-branded category visibility, strengthening the Small Nest Box as the primary acquisition product, and gradually expanding the HenGear catalog.

Growth should remain focused on profitable customer acquisition rather than discount-driven sales volume and should support the broader business without increasing Shopify cannibalization.

02Expand Visibility Within the Roll-Away Nest Box Category
Opportunity

HenGear has successfully established an Amazon presence within the roll-away nesting box niche, but current category share remains relatively small compared to the overall opportunity. Search Query Performance shows that the majority of growth is being driven by non-branded category searches rather than branded demand.

The Q3 focus is to continue expanding visibility among new-to-brand shoppers actively searching for nesting box solutions.

Recommendations
  • Continue expanding visibility across non-branded category searches.
  • Prioritize roll-away nesting box terms where HenGear has a clear competitive advantage.
  • Measure progress through category share growth.
  • Align advertising efforts around profitable NTB acquisition.
03Turn the Small Nest Box Into HenGear's Primary Acquisition Product
Opportunity

The Small Nest Box has clearly emerged as the strongest product in the catalog. It drives the highest sales volume, strongest conversion rates, and most effectively captures category demand. Rather than trying to force equal contribution across all Nest Box sizes, Q3 should focus on maximizing the Small Nest Box's role as the primary entry point into the HenGear catalog.

The goal is to acquire more new customers through the Small Nest Box and use that product as the foundation for upsell opportunities.

Recommendations
  • Continue positioning the Small Nest Box as the entry point product for new-to-brand shoppers.
  • Improve product selection guidance to help customers identify when Medium, Large, or XL is the better fit.
04Expand Beyond Nest Boxes
Opportunity

AutoRoost represents the next step in the HenGear ecosystem. The goal is to acquire new customers through the Small Nest Box and create a natural path toward complementary products that increase customer value over time.

Recommendations
  • Confirm launch timing and inventory readiness.
  • Integrate AutoRoost into cross-sell opportunities across Amazon assets.
  • Integrate AutoRoost into the Store experience.
  • Use AutoRoost as the first step toward a broader product ecosystem strategy.
05Create a Clear Path to the Second Purchase
Opportunity

Store performance has been strong despite receiving no dedicated advertising support, generating meaningful attributed sales and engagement through organic shopper activity.

As the catalog expands, the Store becomes increasingly important as a destination where shoppers can better understand the HenGear product ecosystem, compare solutions, and discover additional products.

Recommendations
  • Shift Store navigation from product-size organization toward solution-based navigation.
  • Improve guidance around selecting the right Nest Box size.
  • Create clearer pathways between Nest Boxes, AutoRoost, and replacement products.
06Advertising Direction
Strategic Direction
  • Continue the current approach: win cheap, high-intent secondary category terms rather than overpaying for head terms, and let organic rank compound from there.
  • Maintain TACOS discipline (recently ~6–7%); prioritize profitable acquisition over raw spend growth.
  • Treat Sponsored Products, Sponsored Brands, and Sponsored Display as a connected funnel rather than three separate channels; feed winning search terms across all three rather than optimizing each in isolation.
  • Build a dedicated PPC structure for AutoRoost now that it's live, mirroring the framework that worked for the Nest Box line.
Sponsored Products
  • Keep Auto campaigns running as the primary discovery engine — combined they're driving ~100 orders at a healthy 10–13% ACOS.
  • Pull back and optimize on Auto Substitutes, currently the account's worst performer (30.46% ACOS, 3.28 ROAS), and reallocate that spend toward SB/SD.
  • Harvest well-performing search terms surfaced from Auto into SP Broad and SP Phrase to start capturing that demand more efficiently and cheaply than Auto does.
  • Once a term proves itself and organic rank movement is visible (the halo effect), graduate it into SP Exact to defend the ranking and capture high-intent traffic at a lower CPC than Broad. SP Exact is currently underweighted relative to its potential.
Sponsored Brands

SB is the most efficient channel in the account at 1.93% ACOS and ~52x ROAS — it is currently capped at a token spend level and should be scaled meaningfully.

  • Scale SB meaningfully given its efficiency advantage.
  • Expand SB Product Collection/Video creative to include AutoRoost alongside Nest Box for cross-sell exposure.
  • Use SB headline ads to reinforce the secondary-keyword ranking push (same terms driving the organic halo effect).
Sponsored Display
  • Push SD with intent rather than pulling back despite the current zero-conversion read — it's early and this is a top-of-funnel/retargeting layer, not a direct-response channel.
  • Feed the same harvested high-performing search terms into SD as product/category targeting (competitor Nest Box ASINs, complementary categories).
  • Test AMC audiences for SD: cart-add/no-purchase, detail-page-view/no-purchase, past Nest Box purchasers (for AutoRoost and Nest Pad cross-sell), and competitor-product viewers.
AutoRoost 2.0
  • Build a dedicated Auto + Broad Modifier campaign structure now that it's live, following the same harvesting pipeline above.
  • Feature AutoRoost in the SB Product Collection ad as a cross-sell off Nest Box traffic.
